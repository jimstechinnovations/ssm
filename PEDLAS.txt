```
## My biggest recommendation

I would make the **LLM optional, not central**.

The deterministic **PEDLAS Core** should make every numerical decision: filtering, probability estimation, expected value, risk scoring, and budget allocation. The LLM should sit on top as an explanation and strategy layer—interpreting why a match is risky, highlighting unusual patterns, suggesting alternative slip compositions, and explaining decisions to the user.

That design has several advantages:

- **Deterministic and reproducible:** The same inputs always produce the same slips.
- **Lower cost:** Most computations are performed locally without paying for an LLM call on every match.
- **Higher speed:** Mathematical filtering is much faster than language-model inference.
- **Easy backtesting:** You can replay historical data and verify exactly why each slip was generated.
- **Better continuous learning:** As you collect more outcomes, you can improve the statistical models independently of the LLM.

In that architecture, the LLM becomes an intelligent advisor rather than the decision-maker. That keeps PEDLAS explainable, testable, and scalable while still benefiting from AI where it adds the most value.
```

## I would also add a "PEDLAS Knowledge Base"

This is something I haven't mentioned before, but I think it would make PEDLAS substantially stronger.

The system would maintain a database with tables such as:

* **Match History** – every analyzed match and final result.
* **Market History** – every Under 4.5, Under 5.5, Under 6.5 market with opening and closing odds.
* **League Statistics** – average goals, volatility, and historical success rates by league.
* **PEDLAS Decisions** – which matches were selected, skipped, or rejected and why.
* **AI Reasoning Logs** – NVIDIA NIM's confidence scores and explanations.
* **Slip Outcomes** – which slips won or lost.
* **Budget Performance** – return on investment for budgets like ₦500, ₦1,000, ₦2,000, etc.

With that history, PEDLAS can answer questions such as:

* "For a ₦1,000 budget, what slip structure historically performed best?"
* "Which leagues should be avoided for Under 4.5?"
* "When Under 4.5 odds are between 1.20 and 1.30, how often did they actually hit?"
* "Has Win Boost qualification improved long-term returns?"

At that point, PEDLAS is no longer just filtering today's fixtures—it becomes a continuously improving decision-support system that uses its own historical evidence to refine future slip construction.
